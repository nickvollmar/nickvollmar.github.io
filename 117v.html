If þeor remains in one place [on the body], make an ointment. Take the ivy that grows on stone and yarrow and the leaf of honeysuckle and cowslip. Pound all well. Lay on a hot stone in a trough. Pour a little of water on [it]. Let steam on that body where there is need. When it is cool, put another hot [stone] on [the body]. Warm [that way] frequently. Soon he is well.

xxxi. Make a good cyst salve. Take wild celery and the foot of a crab and the lower part of wormwood, cowslip, pennyroyal, the leaf of honeysuckle, the leaf of ivy that grows on the ground, the cloved lesser celandine. Pound all. Boil in the grease of a ram or the grease of a buck. Add a third portion of butter. Wring through a cloth. Then add good pitch to [it] and stir until it is cool.
xxxii. Wyrc gode dolh sealfe nim gearwan 7 wudurofan nioþoweard. feldmoran 7 nioþoweardene sigel hweorfan wyl on godre buteran awring þurh clað 7 læt gestandan wel ælc dolh þu meaht lacnian mid.
xxxii. Make a good wound salve. Take yarrow and the lower part of woodruff, wild carrot and the lower part of smooth cat's-ear. Boil in good butter. Wring through a cloth and let stand. You might well heal every wound with [this salve].
