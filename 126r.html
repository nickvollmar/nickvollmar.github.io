Lxv. If person is cut and you shall heal him, see that he is turned to you when you enter, then may he live. If he is turned away, do not greet him with anything. If he may live, boil betony, corncockle, yarrow, pennyroyal, pellitoeries of the wall in butter. Wring through cloth. Let stand. Heat a cup full of warm cow's milk. Put five morsels into that salve. Eat it after a night's fast and eat fresh flesh there where it is fattest and take the salve at night and comfort that wound with old lard or with fresh butter. When it is clean and very red, heal it with that same salve and do not let [the wound] come together39. If it is clean, let it afterwards come together. If it does not better because [of] this leechdom, boil red yarrow and fennel, [and] flax, all equally, in milk. Let boil five times. Wring through a cloth.
