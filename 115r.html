Wyrc briw wiþ lungenadle nim betonican. 7 marubian. wermod. hindheoloþan. wenwyrt nioþoweard. elehtre. elene. rædic eoforþrote. feldmore. gecnua ealle swite wel 7 wyl on buteran 7 awring þurh clað scead on þæt wosberen mela hrer on blede butan fyre oþþæt hit sie swa þicce swa briw ete .iii. snæda mid þy drence wearmes.
Make a pottage against lung disease. Take betony and white horehound, wormwood, hindheal, the lower part of lesser celandine, lupin, radish, stemless carline thistle, wild carrot. Pound all very well and boil in butter and wring through a cloth. Scatter barley meal on that juice. Stir into a bowl [that is] against a flame until it is as thick as a pottage. Eat three morsels with a drink of the warm [pottage].
Eft wyl on hunige anum marubian do hwon beren mela to ete on neahtnestig 7 þonne þu him selle drenc oððe briw sele him hatne 7 læt gerestan þone man æfter tide dæges on þa swiðran sidan 7 hafa þone earm aþened.
Afterwards, boil white horehound alone in honey. Put a little barley meal in [it]. Eat after a night's fast and then you should give him a drink or pottage. Give the hot [thing] to him and let the person rest after a day's time on the right side and have the arm extended.
xv. Wiþ magan wærce wyl wic on cu meolce ado þæt wic of supe hwon wearm sona biþ sel.
xv. Against stomach pain boil, wylch elm in cow milk. Take the wylch elm out. Eat a little warm. Soon [it] is well.
Wiþ aþundenesse 7 man nelle myltan his mete wyl on wætere polleian7 leac cersan sele drincan him biþ sona sel;
Against swelling and the person who cannot digest his food, boil pennyroyal and garlic-mustard in water. Give to drink. Soon he is well.
xvi. Wiþ milte wærcecnua grene sealh rinde seoð on hunige anum sele him etan .iii. snæda on neahtnestig.
xvi. Against spleen pain, pound green willow bark. Boil alone in honey. Give him three morsels to eat after a night's fast.
xvii. Wiþ lenden wærce marudie. nefte. begen emfela ealra do on god ealu wyrc to drence swet hwon sele drincan licge uppe and æfter þon gode hwile.
xvii. Against loin pain, put white horehound, cat's mint, rosemary (all in equal parts) in good ale. Make into a drink. Sweeten a little. Give to drink. Lie face up and [remain like this] afterwards for a good while.
