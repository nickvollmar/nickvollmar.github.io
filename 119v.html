and the bile of a fish and the bile of a crab and the bile of a hare. Mix together. Smear the wound with it. Blow that juice into the wound with a reed. Then pound the leaf of buckthorn. Lay [it] on the wound.

Make an ointment for the same. Take aspen bark and bog-myrtle bark, rowan bark, sloe-thorn bark, bog-myrtle bark, birch bark. Pound all the barks. Boil in cheese whey. Wash with [it] and bathe that limb that the worm is in and after [using] that ointment, dry him and smear with the salve and blow the salve into the wound and lay bramble leaves on [it]. Do that way three times a day in summer and twice [a day] in winter.

[Then] make the black salve if there is need. Gather two pails of cows urine and a pail full of holly bark and ash bark and monk's hood.
