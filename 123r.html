Lviiii. Wiþ þeor wenne gif he sie man on cneowe oþþe on oþrum lime wyrc clam of surre rigenre grut oððe dage do æges hwit to 7 broc cersan lege on þæt lim oþþæt se clam hatige do of þone lege oþerne þær on.
Lviiii. Against a þeor cyst, if it is on the knee or another limb of a person, make a poultice of sour rye grout or dough. Putt the white of an egg and watercress into [it]. Lay [it] on that limb until the poultice heats up. Take [it] off, then lay another there on.
Lx. Wyrc gode ear sealfe hundestunge niþeweard 7 singrene 7 sinfulle. tunhofe nioþoweard. celeþonian leaf. garleac. cropleac. do on win oððe on eced wring þurh hæwenne clað on þæt eare læt standan .iii. niht ær þu hine on do.
Lx. Make a good ear salve. Put the lower part of hound's tongue and bog violet and house leek, [and] the lower part of yard hove, celandine leaf, garlic, [and] leek in wine or in vinegar. Wring through a blue cloth into that ear. Let [the salve] stand three nights before you put it in [the ear].
Eft nim cropleac 7 sinfullan gecnua hwon wines to 7 wring on þæt eare him biþ sona sel.
Afterwards, take leek and house leek. Pound a little [of those herbs] into wine and wring into that ear. Soon he is well.

Lxi. Make a salve against elf kind and night-goers and the devil who has intercourse with people. Take bryony, wormwood, bishopwort, lupine, vervain, henbane, common cudweed, viper's bugloss, crowberry stalk, leek, garlic, heyriffe seed, corncockle, [and] fennel. Put those plants in a cup. Set under an altar. Sing nine masses over it. Boil in butter and in the fat of a sheep. Add a lot of holy salt. Strain through a cloth. Throw the herbs in running water.
