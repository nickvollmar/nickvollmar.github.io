Old English
Modern English
sele drincan scenc fulne.
Give a cup full to drink.
xLii. Gif swið drenc on man gesitte 7 he nelle ofgan nim niþewearde celeþonian 7 lybcornes leaf oþþe arod wyl on ealað do buteran 7 sealt to sele drincan wearmes scenc fulne.
xLii. If a strong drink sits in a person and it will not leave [him], take the lower part of celandine and the leaf of caper spurge or lords-and-ladies. Boil in ale. Put butter and salt into [it]. Give a cup full of the warm [drink] to drink.
xLiii. Wiþ attres drince seoþ henne 7 hocces leaf on wætre ado þone fugel of 7 þa wyrta sele supan þæt broð wel gebuterod swa he hat oft mæge gif he ær hæfþ attor gedruncen ne biþ him ahte þe wyrs gif he þæt broð þonne ær sypð ne meaht þu him þy dæge attor gesellan;
xLiii. Against a drink of poison, seethe a hen and the leaf of common mallow in water. Take the fowl and the herbs from [the water]. Give that broth to eat, well buttered [and] as hot as often he may. If he has drunk poison before, he is none the worse. If he has sipped that broth before then, you may not give him poison that day.
xLiiii. Wiþ lusum sele him etan gesodenne cawel on neaht nestig gelome he biþ lusum bewered.
xLiiii. Against lice, give him soaked cabbage to eat often after a night's fast. He will be defended from lice.
xLv. Gif þorn stinge man on fot oþþe hreod 7 nelle ofgan nime niwe gose tord 7 grene gearwan cnuwige swiþe tosomne clæm on þæt dolh sona biþ sel;
xLv. If a thorn or a reed stings a person on the foot and will not leave, take fresh goose turd and green yarrow. Pound [them] together thoroughly. Smear [them] on that wound. Soon [he] is well.
xLvi. Wiþ æasmælum 7 wiþ eallum eagna wærce ceow wulfescomb wring þonne þurh hæwenne clað wyllenne on þa eagan þæt seap on night þone he restan wille 7 on morgen do æges þæt hwite þær on.
xLvi. Against contractions of the pupil and against all eye pains, chew fuller's teasel, then wring that juice through a blue woolen cloth onto the eyes at night when he wishes to rest, and in the morning put egg whites on [them].
xLvii. Wiþ lyftadle gif se muð sie woh oþþe won nim cellendran gnid on wifes meolce do on þæt hale eare
xLvii. Against paralysis, if the mouth is bent or livid, take celandine. Grind [it] in the milk of a woman. Put on the whole31 ear.
31. As in healthy. ↩
