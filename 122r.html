xLviii. Drenc wiþ ficadle nim bulut 7 eoforþrotan nioþowearde 7 wudufillan 7 geaces suran 7 æferþan gescearfa þas wyrta tosomne do on gellet innan læt standan neahterne ær þu hine drince.
xLviii. A drink against fig disease33. Take black horehound and the lower part of stemless carline thistle and wild chervil and wood sorrel and æferþan. Cut the herbs together. Put inside a large cup. Let stand overnight before you drink it.
Wyrc beþinge nim þæt reade ryden do on trig hæt þonne stanas swiþe hate lege on þæt trig innan 7 he sitte on stole ofer þære beþinge þæt hio hine mæge tela gereocan 7 bewreoh þæt sio hæto mæge hine tela gereocan þonne feallað þa fic wyrmas on þa beþinge him biþ sona sel drince þone drenc ær þære beþinge gif he þonne þa beþinge þurhteon ne mæge drince þone drenc ælce dæge oþþæt him sel sie.
Make an ointment. Take the red ryden. Put on a tray then heat [some] stones very hot. Lay [the stones] on the tray and he should sit on a stool over that ointment so that it may smoke well and cover him so that heat smokes well him, then the fig worms will fall in the ointment. Soon he is well. Drink the drink [for fig disease] before the ointment. If he then may not carry through the ointment, drink that drink each day until he is well.
xLviiii. Wiþ sculdor wærce 7 earma wyl betonican on ealoð sele drincan gelome 7 simle smire hine æt fyre mid wenwyrte.
xLviiii. Against shoulder and arm pain, boil betony in ale. Give [it] to drink often and always smear him by the fire with lesser celandine.
L. Gif cneow sar sie cnua belenan 7 hemlic beþe mid 7 lege on.
L. If the knee is sore, pound henbane and hemlock. Foment34 with [it] and lay [it] on.
Li. Gif se fot sar sie ellen leaf 7 wegbrædan 7 mucgwyrt gecnua 7 lege on 7 gebind hat þær on.
Li. If the foot is sore, pound elder leaf and greater plantain and mugwort and lay [them] on [the foot] and bind [it] hot there on.
Lii. Gif þu ne mæge blod dolh forwriþan nim niwe horses tord adrig on sunnan gegnid to duste
Lii. If you may not stanch a blood wound, take a fresh turd of a horse. Dry it in the sun. Grind to dust
