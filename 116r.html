Wyrc briw to þon ilcan wuducunellan. hleomoc bewyl þara meolce þriddan dæl þære wyrte of þam meolcum scead hwæten mela þær on 7 ete þone briw cealdne 7 supe þa meoluc him bið sona sel gif se briw 7 se drenc inne gewuniað þu meaht þone man gelacnian gif him offleogeð him bið selre þæt þu hine na ne grete him biþ his feorh adl getenge.
Make a pottage for the same [disease]. Breckland thyme, brooklime. Boil a third of the milk. [Take] the herbs from the milk. Shed wheat meal thereon and eat that cold pottage and sup the milk. Soon he is well. If the pottage and the drink remain in [him], you might heal that person. If it flees him, it is better that you not treat him. His life disease is near him.
xxiii. Gif wyrmas beoþ on mannes innoðe wyl on buteran grene rudan drinc on neaht nestig scenc fulne hi gewitað ealle aweg mid þy utgange 7 he bið sona hal;
xxiii. If worms are in the gut of a person, boil green pennyroyal in butter. Drink a cup full after a night's fast. They all depart away with the evacuation and soon he is whole.
To þon ilcan genim cymenes dust meng to gate geallan 7 fearres gnid þone nafolan mid ealle hi gewitaþ niþer; of þæm men
For the same, take the dust of cumin. Mix into the bile of a goat and the bile of a bull. Rub the navel with i[it]. They all depart downward out that person.
xxiiii. Wiþ lið wærce sing .viiii. siþum þis gealdor þær on 7 þin spatle spiw on: Malignus obligauit angel us curauit dominus saluauit. him biþ sona sel.
xxiiii. Against joint pain, sing this chant nine times thereon and spit your spittle on it, "Malingus obligavit angel us curavit angelus curavit dominus salvavit." Soon he is well.
To þon ilcan genim culfran tord 7 gate tord drige swiðe 7 gnid to duste meng wiþ hunig 7 wiþ buteran smire mid þa leoþu.
For the same, take pigeon dung and goat dung. Dry fully and grind to dust. Mix with honey and with butter. Smear the joints with [it].
xxv. Wiþ weartum genim hundes micgean 7 muse blod meng tosomne smire þa weartan hi gewitaþ sona aweg.
xxv. Against warts, take the urine of a hound and the blood of a mouse. Mix together. Smear the warts. They will depart away soon.
