Modern English
7 do on þa eagan siþþan
and put it on the eyes afterwards.
Wyrc gode drige scade sealfe nim swegles æppel 7 gebærned sealt 7 pipor 7 attrum 7 hwit cwudu gegnid to duste asift þurh clað do lytlum on.
Make a good dry shed-salve. Take beetle nut and burned salt and pepper and with bile and mastic. Grind to dust. Sift through a cloth. Put a little on [him].
Eft hwit cwudu 7 gebærned ofter syl gnid to duste 7 nytta swa þe þearf sie ægþer mæg adon flie of eagan.
Afterwards, grind mastic and burned oyster shell to dust and use as needed. Either may remove the white speck from the eyes.
Wyrc smeþe eag sealfe nim buteran wyl on wannan a fleot þæt fam of 7 ahlyttre þa buteran on blede do eft þæt hluttre on wannan gecnua celeþonian bisceopwyrt wudumerce wyl swiþe aseoh þurh clað nytta swa þe þearf sie;
Make a smooth eye salve. Take butter, boil it in a pan. Skim off the foam and clarify the butter in a bowl. Put afterwards that clarified [butter] in a pan. Pound celandine, bishopwort and wild celery. Boil vigorously. Strain through a cloth. Use as needed.
III. Wiþ ear wærce genim henne gelyndo 7 ofter scylle sete on gleda gewyrm hwon 7 dryp on þa earan sona beoð hale;
III. Against ear pain, take hen's grease and an oyster shell. Set on live coals. Warm a little and drip in the ears. Soon [they] are whole.
Eft celendran seap 7 wifes meoluc gewyrm on scylle 7 dryp on þa earan.
Afterwards, warm the sap of coriander and the milk of a woman in the shell and drip in the ears.
Gif wyrmas sien on earan do belenan seap wearm on þa wyrmas hie beoþ deade 7 feallað of 7 þa earan hale.
If worms are in the ears, put warm henbane sap on the worms. They will be dead and will fall off and the ears will be whole.
Eft wring curmeallan seap on oþþe marubian oððe wermod wearmne. Sona him bið sel.
Afterwards, wring knapweed sap or white horehound or warm wormwood. Soon he is well.
Wyrc gode earsealfe genim bares geallan 7 fearres 7 ele ealra emfela læt drypan wearn on þæt eare.
Make a good ear salve. Take the bile of a bear and the bile of a bull and oil, all in equal parts. Let drip the warm [salve] on that ear.
